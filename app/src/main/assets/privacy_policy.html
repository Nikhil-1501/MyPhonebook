We collect different types of information about you, including information that may directly identify you, information that is about you, but individually does not personally identify you, and information that we combine with our other users. This includes information that we collect directly from you or through automated collection technologies.

III. Data Collection Technologies

The technologies we use for this automatic data collection may include:

  • Cookies (or browser cookies): A “cookie” is a small file placed on the hard drive of your computer or mobile device. They allow us to recognize you when you return to our Website and Application.

IV. Security Measures and Access Control

To maintain appropriate levels of access by internal workers, a variety of security measures will be instituted as recommended by the Security Official and approved by Doc.com. Mechanisms to control access to PHI (Protected Health Information) include:

  • Authorization: Access will be user-based, whereby users of a system gain access based upon the identity of the user.
  • Identification/Authentication: Unique user identification (user id) and authentication is required for all systems that maintain or access PHI. Users will be held accountable for all actions performed on the system.
    • Authentication shall be by strictly controlled passwords.
    • The user must secure his/her authentication control (e.g., password) such that it is known only to that user.
    • An automatic timeout re-authentication is required after a certain period of no activity.
    • The workstation must freeze after three unsuccessful attempts to gain access.
  • Transmission Security: Technical security mechanisms are in place to guard against unauthorized access to data transmitted over networks.
    • Encryption shall be utilized for emails where electronic PHI is transmitted.
  • Remote Access: Access into Doc.com's network from outside will be granted using approved devices and pathways via a “virtual private network” (VPN).

V. Third-Party Information Use

We do not control the collection and use of your information collected by third parties. When possible, these organizations are under contractual obligations to use this data only for providing the services to us and to maintain this information strictly confidential. These third parties may, however, aggregate the information they collect with information from their other customers for their own purposes.

VI. Children Under the Age of 18

No one under age 18 may provide any information to or through the Website or Application. We do not knowingly collect Personal Data from children under 18. If you are under the age of 18, your parent or legal guardian must create the account, submit your personal information, and agree to the Terms of Use and the Privacy Policy on your behalf. If we learn we have collected Personal Data from a child under 18 without verification of parental consent, we will delete that information.
